Understanding Trademarks in Intellectual Property Rights (IPR)

Design Rights


Design in Intellectual Property Rights is a creative arrangement of visual elements like shapes, colours, and patterns.
Designs are protected under IPR through design patents.

Examples of design patent can be:

• a unique chair with a distinctive shape and color,
• a decorative pattern on t-shirt,
• blueprint of a house,
• unique pattern for a smartphone case, (but a new type of smart phone is not a example of design patent)
• unique and stylish logo, etc.
 The primary goal of protecting a design through IPR is to encourage innovation and creativity by giving creators rights to their designs.
 Types of Design Protection are:
• Industrial Design
• Fashion Design
 Once registered, the design owner has exclusive rights to use, make, sell, or license the design for a specified period.
 Protecting a design is important to prevent others from copying and profiting from your unique design.
 The duration of design protection varies by country but typically lasts for 20 years from the date of registration.
 Importance of Design are Aesthetic Appeal, Functionality, Brand Identity.
 Design infringement occurs when someone copies or reproduces a protected design without permission from the owner.
 Two different people can’t have a design patent for the same design.
 Design is all around us, from the layout of a smartphone's user interface to the shape of a chair we sit on.
